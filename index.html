Artista multidisciplinario

Isaac Ulam (nacido el 8 de junio de 1978 en Blanes, Girona, España) es un reconocido artista multidisciplinar cuyo trabajo abarca el cine, la interpretación, el guionismo, la música, la fotografía y las artes visuales. Su carrera se caracteriza por un enfoque innovador que trasciende las fronteras convencionales entre las disciplinas artísticas.

Obtuvo un reconocimiento notable con su película debut, Bronko (2019), que fue preseleccionada para representar a España en la 93.ª edición de los Premios Oscar, recibió diez nominaciones en los Premios Goya 2021 y una nominación a Mejor Película en los XIII Premios Gaudí.

Su producción artística, que incluye pinturas, fotografías, ilustraciones y piezas audiovisuales, ha sido presentada en galerías de arte, ferias internacionales y prestigiosas colecciones privadas.

Educación y primeros años Hijo de Alfonso Serrano Bou y Rosa María Calleja de la Fuente, ambos trabajadores de la industria textil, Ulam creció en un entorno que fomentaba la creatividad. Su padre, además de su profesión principal, era pintor, escritor, músico e instructor de yoga, y fundó el Centro de Yoga Suryanamaskar en Blanes en 1961. Este ambiente familiar enriqueció la sensibilidad artística de Ulam desde muy joven.

During his youth, he developed a critical perspective toward consumerism, which influenced his early visual works exploring themes of social marginality. A turning point in his development was his encounter, at age 17, with Chilean writer Roberto Bolaño, then a resident of Blanes. This relationship evolved into a significant friendship, culminating in Ulam's participation in a tribute to Bolaño held in Girona, an event attended by prominent figures such as Patti Smith, Juan Villoro, and Jorge Herralde.

Academic Background

Formal recognition of Ulam's talent came in 2002 when he was selected as one of ten emerging talents in experimental cinema at the prestigious Sitges International Film Festival for his audiovisual work POETIZE. This recognition propelled his academic training in film direction at IDEP Barcelona, a reference institution in the Spanish audiovisual field.

His creative potential was further endorsed when the Antoni Tàpies Foundation commissioned him to create an environmental art piece, a project that consolidated his position in the contemporary Catalan artistic landscape.

Artistic Philosophy and Influences

Isaac Ulam's artistic expression is distinguished by a distinctive fusion of Mediterranean folk, psychedelic elements, mystical imagery, and sharp social critique. Critics have described his music as "one of the most extraordinary expressions of Catalan singer-songwriting in recent years."

His work is deeply nourished by literature, connection with nature, and existential reflection. Ulam constructs his musical compositions and visual art from a poetic and intuitive approach, establishing a dialogue between interior emotional landscapes and Mediterranean spirituality. As he has expressed: "I make songs to open spaces that don't yet exist."

Roberto Bolaño's influence on his artistic development was decisive. For almost two decades, their conversations on the Blanes seafront about art, literature, and society nurtured Ulam's creative vision. A significant anecdote that the artist often shares recounts how, upon expressing his admiration for Bolaño's work published by Editorial Anagrama, the writer responded: "First write your own book, then you can read mine." Ulam has repeatedly stated that he has honored that commitment ever since.

After Bolaño's death, poet Jorge Morales invited Ulam to participate in a commemorative event at the Municipal Theater of Girona, an event that brought together figures of international relevance such as poet and musician Patti Smith, Mexican writer Juan Villoro, and editor Jorge Herralde.

Film Career

Bronko: Debut Film

In 2019, Isaac Ulam made his directorial debut with the feature film Bronko, simultaneously assuming the roles of screenwriter, producer, and protagonist. The film articulates an ascetic vision of an individual who frontally rejects the prevailing social model, opting for a path of personal self-destruction. Through themes such as loneliness, survival, and human sacrifice, the protagonist Roman Di Martino "Bronko" emerges as a symbol of the contemporary redeemer.

The world premiere of Bronko took place in December 2019 at Cine Noir in Brooklyn, New York, an alternative space dedicated to screening avant-garde and independent cinema, constituting the ideal environment for the presentation of this inaugural work.

The media impact of the film included its mention in The New York Times, a publication that highlighted the uniqueness of the exhibition space and its commitment to independent cinema.
